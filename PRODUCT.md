# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

- **Recruiters & hiring managers** (CTOs, tech leads, senior devs) — scan fast, looking for evidence of skill depth and shipped work. They spend 5-30 seconds before deciding whether to reach out.
- **Potential freelance clients** — evaluating professionalism, design taste, and ability to deliver polished products. They compare against other portfolios and agencies.
- **Developer peers** — technical curiosity about architecture, stack choices, and open-source contributions.

All three audiences share one context: they arrive via LinkedIn, GitHub, Google, or direct referral, and they judge the portfolio as a proxy for the developer.

## Product Purpose

Convert passive visits into active opportunities: interview invitations, freelance inquiries, collaboration offers, and professional network growth. The portfolio is a 24/7 first impression that must communicate competence, taste, and personality in under 10 seconds, then reward deeper exploration with substance (case studies, live demos, source code).

Success is measured by: contact form submissions, demo/repo click-throughs, time-on-site for case studies, and recruiter follow-ups.

## Brand Personality

**Technical confidence + editorial restraint.** The voice of someone who knows their craft deeply and doesn't need to shout about it. Warm but direct, precise but not cold. Three words: _crafted, intentional, distinctive._

The emotional goal is **trust through demonstrated competence** — the visitor should think "this person ships quality work" without being told.

## Anti-references

- **AI-generic developer portfolios** — template cards, identical layouts, purple/blue gradient clichés, Inter font default, three equal feature columns, lorem ipsum project descriptions.
- **Agency overdesign** — scroll-hijacking, excessive animations, illegible experimental typography, form over function.
- **Forgettable minimalism** — so restrained it becomes invisible. No personality, no distinguishing marks, indistinguishable from a portfolio generator output.
- **Tech-bro aesthetics** — dark mode with neon green accents, matrix references, "hacker" vibes. Confident but not performative.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The portfolio must demonstrate the skills it claims — if I say I build polished UIs, the portfolio IS that proof.
2. **Show, don't tell.** Projects, demos, and code carry more weight than adjectives. Every claim is backed by visible evidence.
3. **Dark-first, not dark-only.** The default mode is dark (identity), but light mode has its own personality — not just inverted colors.
4. **Micro-interactions with purpose.** Motion must communicate: hierarchy, feedback, state transitions. Never "because it looks cool."
5. **Anti-generic at every touchpoint.** Every design decision must survive the question: "could this appear in a no-code portfolio template?" If yes, rework it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Target:** WCAG 2.2 AA across all pages and both themes.
- **Known accommodations:** `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on all animations; visible focus rings for keyboard navigation; `aria-labelledby` on landmark sections; skip-to-content link as first focusable element.
- **Language:** Bilingual ES/EN with proper `hreflang` tags. Language switcher preserves current route.
- **Contrast:** Body text ≥ 4.5:1 against background in both dark and light modes.
